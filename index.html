Matlab + Simulink Model of an Industrial Cooling Loop

Dynamic simulation of a water–glycol cooling circuit for an industrial drivetrain test facility using Simscape Fluids and Simulink.

Project Context and Objectives

The goal of this model is to size and tune a closed-loop cooling system that removes heat from test-bench components while keeping supply temperatures and pressures inside defined limits.

The plant is represented with Simscape Fluids blocks for pumps, valves, pipework, and a plate heat exchanger, while supervisory logic and PID controllers are implemented in Simulink.

System Topology and Results

The circuit consists of a main circulation pump, distribution manifold, consumer branches, and a plate heat exchanger. The working fluid is a water–glycol mixture.

End-Effector for High-Voltage Power Modules

Mechanical design of a robotic gripper for 1000 V DC applications.

Project Abstract

This project involves the development of a mechanically guided, electrically insulated end-effector that enables safe and repeatable contacts to high-voltage (HV) power modules during manufacturing and laboratory tests[cite: 9].

The tool replaces manual test leads, which are slow to position, operator-dependent, and often marginal on creepage/clearance [cite: 16-19]. The design utilizes fork-type guides and spring contacts to achieve positionally consistent insertion without side-loading the device terminals[cite: 11].

Design Specifications

The end-effector is designed to handle DC-DC residential energy converters and AC-DC fast-charging stages[cite: 10].

Voltage Max
1000 V DC [cite: 54]
Current (Nominal)
150 A [cite: 52]
Peak Current (3s)
300 A [cite: 53]
Clearance
≥ 12 mm [cite: 55]

Mechanical Architecture & Images

The system uses two U-shaped aluminum forks with tapered entries to guide the tool onto the DUT (Device Under Test)[cite: 72]. To prevent damage to the copper lugs or plastic housing, the tip radius is set to 1.5 mm[cite: 76].

A visual alignment mark (green stripe) on the front serves as a "seat line" to indicate when the tool is fully engaged and safe to energize[cite: 70].

Safety and Verification

To mitigate the risk of shorting adjacent HV rails, the design includes insulating fork caps so only the inner spring is live[cite: 115].

Verification steps include a dimensional check of fork spacing vs. DUT plastic, contact resistance measurements at 100 A (target $<100~\mu\Omega$), and a dielectric withstand test at 2 kV DC for 1 minute [cite: 120-123].

Design & Construction of PCBA Tester

Internship project at Tesla Giga Berlin · ECU ICT/FCT fixture with strain-safe touchdown

Project Scope

Designed and assembled a semi-automated In-Circuit Test (ICT) and Functional Circuit Test (FCT) fixture for automotive Electronic Control Units (ECUs). The system required precise mechanical alignment to engage pogo-pins with the PCBA without inducing excessive mechanical stress.

Strain Analysis & Validation

To ensure the PCBA was not damaged during the "touchdown" phase (clamping), I performed a strain analysis using rosette strain gauges.

Strain data was acquired using a NI DAQ system and processed in LabVIEW to verify that principal strains remained within the IPC-9704 guidelines, preventing solder ball cracking on sensitive BGA components.

Software & Control

The test sequence was automated using LabVIEW, which controlled the pneumatic actuators for the fixture lid and communicated with the ECU via CAN bus to trigger functional diagnostics.
